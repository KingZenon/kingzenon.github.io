Description

Early life and education

Born in Madrid, Spain, on May 8, 1975, Enrique Iglesias is the son of singer Julio Iglesias and journalist Isabel Preysler. Following a kidnapping threat against his grandfather in 1983, he moved to Miami, Florida. Raised largely by his family's nanny, he later attended Gulliver Preparatory School and studied Business Administration at the University of Miami.

Career

Enrique secretly began writing music and recording demo tapes in his teens under the pseudonym Enrique Martinez. In 1995, he dropped out of the University of Miami to release his eponymous debut album, Enrique Iglesias. The album sold a million copies in just three months and earned him a Grammy Award. Follow-up Spanish albums like Vivir (1997) and Cosas del Amor (1998) solidified his status as the best-selling Latin act of the decade.In 1999, his track "Bailamos"—featured in the film Wild Wild West—became a massive number-one hit and launched his global crossover. This led to his first English-language album, Enrique (1999), which featured a duet with Whitney Houston. He cemented his international superstar status with the 2001 multi-platinum album Escape, which included his signature hit "Hero".Throughout the 2000s and 2010s, Iglesias consistently topped the charts in both languages. His discography features acclaimed Spanish records like Quizás (2002), which won a Latin Grammy, as well as bilingual and dance-pop projects like Euphoria (2010) and Sex and Love (2014). He expanded his musical repertoire to include reggaeton and urban fusions, scoring record-breaking hits such as "Bailando" and "I Like It". He has achieved more number-one songs on the Billboard Hot Latin Tracks chart than any other male artist.
